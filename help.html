Getting Started

The Legal Document Analyzer helps you understand complex legal documents by highlighting important clauses, simplifying legal terminology, and identifying potential issues.

1

Navigate to a Legal Document

Open any webpage that contains a legal document or contract.

2

Click the Extension Icon

Click the Legal Document Analyzer icon in your Chrome toolbar to open the extension.

3

Analyze the Document

Click the "Analyze Document" button to begin the analysis process.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is my document information secure?

Yes. The Legal Document Analyzer processes all documents locally in your browser. No document content is sent to external servers or stored online unless you explicitly enable cloud processing in the settings.

What types of documents can be analyzed?

The extension works best with contracts, terms of service, privacy policies, and other legal documents displayed as HTML on webpages. For PDF analysis, the document needs to be viewed in Chrome's built-in PDF viewer.

How accurate is the analysis?

While our tool uses advanced pattern recognition to identify legal concepts, it is designed as an assistant rather than a replacement for professional legal advice. Always consult with a qualified attorney for important legal matters.

Does it work on all websites?

The analyzer works on most websites, but some sites with unusual document formatting or heavy JavaScript may not be fully compatible. For best results, use it on standard HTML pages or PDF documents.

Can I customize the analysis?

Yes. Use the toggle switches in the extension popup to enable or disable specific features. Additional customization options are available in the Settings page.

Troubleshooting

The extension isn't highlighting anything

  • Make sure you're on a page with legal text (at least a few paragraphs).
  • Try refreshing the page and running the analysis again.
  • Check that you have the highlighting feature enabled in the extension popup.

The analysis is taking too long

  • For very long documents, analysis may take longer to complete.
  • Try disabling some features (like the language simplification) to speed up the process.
  • Check your internet connection if you have cloud processing enabled.

The extension crashed or isn't responding

  • Reload the page and try again.
  • Restart your browser if the issue persists.
  • Check for extension updates in the Chrome Web Store.
